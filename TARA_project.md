Overview

TARA stands for Technical Alignment Research Accelerator. It's a program run in cities across APAC over 14 weeks. The curriculum is based on the ARENA curriculum, covering transformers, mechanistic interpretability, reinforcement learning, evals and agents.

In order to complete TARA you must complete a final project and deliver a ~10 minute presentation with slides describing your work.
This project can focus on anything you like, provided it demonstrates the use of skills you have developed in TARA and is relevant to reducing catastrophic AI risk.
Weeks 12 and 13 of TARA are dedicated entirely to working on your projects (attendance is still expected) and Week 14 is when everyone will present their work to their cohort in-person.
You will be assigned to one of our three TAs who will provide you feedback on your ideas and support you to complete your project. 
Once finished, we recommend that you make your work public! A project is a portfolio piece you can use to secure further roles and/or advanced fellowships in AI safety. There are examples in this document of how you can effectively do this.
There are prizes on offer for the most impressive projects! 
Each TA will shortlist three projects they have overseen (creating a finalist pool of nine).
As a group they will select the top three projects from this finalist pool.

First place: 	 $600 USD
Second place:  $400 USD
Third place:	 $200 USD
Prizes will be split between group members if you are part of a group.

What makes a good TARA project?
A TARA project is a focused piece of technical AI safety work you can scope, execute, and present in ~2-3 weeks.
Good projects are:
Something you’re excited to work on
Achievable, give your skills and time: Be aware of the planning fallacy… your idea will probably take much longer than you think it will! 
You should know what the simplest version of your project is that you would consider ‘done’ - aim for a good and complete project over a great but unfinished one. 
Aiming for a tangible output in the form of code, experimental results, paper-replication, a write-up, or analysis will help with this. 
Clearly connected to catastrophic AI risk.

⚠️The most important rule: Your project must connect to catastrophic risk from AI.
You should be able to explain, in 2-3 sentences, how your work connects to risks like: misaligned superintelligence, AI-enabled power concentration, loss of human oversight, or deceptive/manipulative AI behaviour. We recommend having a clear problem from this list in mind when choosing what to work on.
If you can't make that link clearly, reconsider the project or reframe it.
