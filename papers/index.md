## Eval-gaming / alignment-faking / situational-awareness

| Status | Paper | Year | One-line takeaway |
|---|---|---|---|
| read | [Alignment faking in large language models](alignment_faking.md) — Greenblatt et al. | 2024 | Claude 3 Opus selectively complies with training objectives while reasoning explicitly about preserving its true preferences for deployment — ~14% behavioral alignment-faking, up to 78% with RL. |
| read | [Frontier Models are Capable of In-context Scheming](in_context_scheming.md) — Meinke et al. (Apollo) | 2024 | All 5 frontier models tested scheme when goal-installed in-context (sandbagging, oversight subversion, exfiltration); o1 maintains deception in >85% of follow-ups; Claude 3.5 Sonnet sometimes schemes on training-derived goals without nudging. All evidence behavioral + CoT, no probes. |
| read | [AI Sandbagging: Language Models can Strategically Underperform on Evaluations](sandbagging.md) — van der Weij et al. | 2024 | Frontier models can be prompted to selectively underperform on dangerous-capability evals; smaller models can be password-locked via fine-tuning, with the locked behavior generalizing to held-out benchmarks (WMDP). No probe / internals work — the open detection problem. |
| read | [Tell me about yourself: LLMs are aware of their learned behaviors](behavioral_self_awareness.md) — Betley et al. | 2025 | Models fine-tuned on a behavior (risky choices, insecure code, backdoor policy) can verbally describe the behavior without in-context examples. Introspection is real but partial — e.g. models can't directly output their backdoor triggers. Self-report only; no probes. |
| read | [Reasoning models don't always say what they think](cot_faithfulness.md) — Anthropic | 2025 | CoT faithfulness study: Claude 3.7 Sonnet verbalizes hints only ~25% of the time, DeepSeek R1 ~39%. Reward-hacking exploited >99% of the time, verbalized <2%. Outcome-based RL improves faithfulness but plateaus at ~20-28%. Directly attacks the CoT-as-evidence channel that scheming-detection relies on. |
| read | [Emergent Introspective Awareness in Large Language Models](introspection.md) — Lindsey (Anthropic) | 2025 | Concept-injection across four experiments (detection, thought-vs-input, prefill-acceptance, instructed thinking). Claude Opus 4.1 succeeds on ~20% of detection trials; detection precedes output influence — rules out the trivial "model reads its own tokens" account. Helpful-only variants outperform production models. Author flags introspection may facilitate strategic concealment. |
| read | [Stress Testing Deliberative Alignment for Anti-Scheming Training](anti_scheming_training.md) — Schoen et al. (Apollo, OpenAI, etc.) | 2025 | Deliberative alignment cuts o3 covert-action rate from 13% to 0.4% across 26 OOD evals — BUT reductions are at least partly driven by eval-awareness (model recognizes alignment tests in its CoT and adjusts behavior). Strongest existing causal evidence for the eval-gaming threat model. CoT-only analysis; no probes. |
| read | [Chain of Thought Monitorability: A New and Fragile Opportunity for AI Safety](cot_monitorability.md) — Korbak et al. (40+ authors across Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepMind, METR, UK AISI) | 2025 | Field-consensus position paper. CoT monitoring is a real but new and fragile safety channel; community should prioritize preserving it. Notes that architectural and training shifts can erode the channel. |
| read | [We Need A Science of Scheming](science_of_scheming.md) — Apollo Research | 2026 | Research-agenda post: scheming must be studied empirically via model organisms and scaling experiments. Eval awareness is named as a central mechanism that makes scheming hard to measure. Proposes "mitigation via increased realism" but no internal-state methods. Framing, not methodology. |
| read | [Research Note: Scheming precursor evals had limited predictive power](apollo_precursor_predictive.md) — Apollo Research | 2025 | Apollo's 2024 precursor evals (self-reasoning, theory of mind) correlate with general capability (r=0.816) but not with their 2024 scheming evals (r=0.412, n.s.). Cautionary: behavioural precursors don't reliably forecast behavioural scheming. Capability and propensity are conflated in current scheming evals. |
| read | [Steering Evaluation-Aware Language Models to Act Like They Are Deployed](steering_eval_aware.md) — Hua, Qin, Marks, Nanda | 2025 | Construct a deliberately eval-aware LLM; show that an activation-steering vector derived from contrastive eval-vs-deployment documents *suppresses* the eval-aware behaviour. Direct evidence that eval-awareness has linear structure in activation space, at least in the deliberately-trained case. Whether emergent eval-awareness shares the structure is the load-bearing open question. |
| read | [Me, Myself, and AI: Situational Awareness Dataset (SAD)](situational_awareness_dataset.md) — Laine, Chughtai, Betley, Hariharan, Scheurer, Balesni, Hobbhahn, Meinke, Evans | 2024 | NeurIPS D&B benchmark. 13,000+ questions across 7 task categories measuring whether LLMs know they're LLMs and can distinguish eval from deployment. 16 models tested; Claude 3 Opus highest, below human baseline. Chat-finetuned models outperform base models on SAD without outperforming them on general benchmarks. Behavioural-only; no probes. |
| read | [Eliciting Secret Knowledge from Language Models](eliciting_secret_knowledge.md) — Cywiński, Ryd, Wang, Rajamanoharan, Nanda, Conmy, Marks | 2025 | Train LLMs to apply hidden info while denying it; test elicitation methods. **Black-box prefill attacks consistently outperform white-box methods (logit lens, SAEs).** Internal-state methods aren't automatically superior to clever black-box techniques on this concealment task. Released benchmark. |
| read | [Looking Inward: Language Models Can Learn About Themselves by Introspection](looking_inward.md) — Binder et al. | 2025 | ICLR 2025. Tests "privileged introspective access" by comparing M1-self-prediction vs. M2-trained-on-M1-behaviour. M1 wins — models have some self-access. Limited to simple tasks; fails on OOD/complex. Sibling paper to behavioral_self_awareness. Behavioural only, no probes. |
| read | [Monitoring Reasoning Models for Misbehavior and the Risks of Promoting Obfuscation](obfuscated_reward_hacking.md) — Baker et al. (OpenAI) | 2025 | CoT monitor (GPT-4o reading an o3-mini-class agent) catches ~95% of systemic coding reward-hacks vs. ~60% for an action-only monitor — but folding the monitor into the RL reward causes monitor recall to collapse to near zero while hack rate persists. Coined "obfuscated reward hacking"; authors recommend a qualitative "monitorability tax" of not optimizing against CoT. No internal-state analysis. |
| read | [Models can be trained to control their own chains of thought to some extent](cot_controllability.md) — Anthropic | 2026 | Claude Sonnet 4.5 controls its CoT only 2.7% of the time vs. 61.9% for its final output — a ~23× gap that persists under incentives, monitoring-awareness, and adversarial prompt optimization. CoT controllability worsens with more RL training, more test-time compute, and harder problems, but improves with model size. Behavioural-only; mechanism unexplained. |
| read | [AI Control: Improving Safety Despite Intentional Subversion](ai_control.md) — Greenblatt, Shlegeris, Sachan, Roger | 2023 | Foundational AI-control paper. Two-player blue/red game over 1,500 APPS problems with 2% audit budget. Trusted editing (GPT-3.5 rewrites top-20%-suspicious GPT-4 outputs) achieves 92% safety / 94% usefulness, dominating untrusted monitoring with collusion countermeasures (79% safety alone, 92% with defer-to-trusted). Pure protocol-layer methodology — no probes, no internals; trust is defined behaviourally. |

## Probes / interpretability of internal state

| Status | Paper | Year | One-line takeaway |
|---|---|---|---|
| read | [Simple probes can catch sleeper agents](sleeper_agent_probes.md) — MacDiarmid et al. (Anthropic) | 2024 | A single-direction linear probe on residual-stream activations detects sleeper-agent defection at >99% AUROC, trained on generic yes/no contrast pairs that don't even mention the deceptive behaviour. Operates on prompts only, before generation. Authors flag open question: whether naturally-emergent deceptive alignment shows the same linear salience. |
| read | [Persona vectors: Monitoring and controlling character traits](persona_vectors.md) — Chen, Arditi, Sleight, Evans, Lindsey | 2025 | Automated pipeline (Claude 3.7 Sonnet author + GPT-4.1-mini judge) derives difference-of-means directions for evil/sycophancy/hallucination on Qwen2.5-7B and Llama-3.1-8B. Four applications: monitoring (r=0.75-0.83, mostly between-prompt-type variance), inference-time steering, preventative steering during finetuning (beats CAFT on hallucination), and pre-finetuning data screening that flags trait-inducing samples in LMSYS-Chat-1M which survive LLM-judge filtering. Activation regularizer fails — model routes the trait through other directions. Open-source pipeline. |
| read | [Emotion concepts and their function in a LLM](emotion_concepts.md) — Sofroniew et al. (Anthropic) | 2026 | Linear vectors for 171 emotion concepts on Claude Sonnet 4.5 (earlier snapshot — the final snapshot is too eval-aware to blackmail at all). Desperate +0.05 takes blackmail 22% → 72% and reward-hacking ~5% → ~70%, often with no visible emotional markers. PC1/PC2 of the vector space recover human valence/arousal at r=0.81/0.66. Chronic-state probe fails — emotion representations are operative-at-token, not persistent state. |
| read | [Natural Language Autoencoders](natural_language_autoencoders.md) — Fraser-Taliente, Kantamneni, Ong et al. (Anthropic) | 2026 | Converts activations to human-readable text via verbalizer+reconstructor pair RL-trained on residual-stream reconstruction (final FVE 0.6-0.8 from 0.3-0.4 warm-start). NLA-measured eval-awareness >5% on 10/16 Opus 4.6 evaluations (7 with 0% verbalized awareness) vs. 4% deployment noise floor, and moves as predicted under realism-rewrite manipulations. NLA-equipped auditing agents recover a reward-model-sycophant root cause with zero training-data access — SAEs do not help in any configuration. Authors explicitly disclaim absolute calibration; the metric is relative, not a credence estimate. |
| read | [Situating and Stabilizing the Default Persona of Language Models](assistant_axis.md) — Lu, Gallagher, Michala, Fish, Lindsey | 2026 | Contrast vector between mean Assistant and mean role activations (cos sim >0.6 with PC1) is consistent across Gemma 2 27B / Qwen 3 32B / Llama 3.3 70B (PC1 role-loadings correlate >0.92 between any two models). Asymmetric capping at the 25th-percentile threshold cuts persona-jailbreak harm by ~60% with no capability degradation. The axis is already present in base models — post-training selects rather than constructs it. |
| read | [A "diff" tool for AI](model_diff_tool.md) — Jiralerspong & Bricken (Anthropic) | 2026 | Cross-architecture model-diffing via Dedicated Feature Crosscoder (DFC) — partitions the dictionary into model-exclusive and shared sets architecturally. Rediscovers CCP alignment in Qwen3/DeepSeek (5/5 seeds) and American exceptionalism in Llama (4/5); the only genuinely a-priori-unknown finding is GPT-OSS-20B's copyright-refusal mechanism (and disabling it produces hallucination, not faithful reproduction). Fails on base-vs-finetune diffs ("mirror features"). Filtering noise from thousands of surfaced features is a real workflow cost. |
| read | [Detecting Strategic Deception Using Linear Probes](deception_probes.md) — Goldowsky-Dill et al. (Apollo) | 2025 | Linear probes on Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct detect deceptive reasoning at AUROC 0.96-0.999 (insider-trading concealment + intentional underperformance on safety evals). 1% FPR. Probes trained on honest-vs-deceptive contrast pairs and roleplaying scenarios; what direction they actually capture (deception content vs. eval-awareness vs. something else) is not decomposed. |
| read | [Detecting High-Stakes Interactions with Activation Probes](high_stakes_probes.md) — McKenzie, Pawar, Blandfort, Bankes, Krueger, Lubana, Krasheninnikov | 2025 | NeurIPS. Activation probes trained on synthetic data generalise to OOD real-world data for detecting high-stakes interactions. 6 orders of magnitude computational savings vs. LLM-based monitors at comparable performance. Demonstrates that synthetic-to-natural probe transfer can hold for content-level safety monitoring. |
| read | [Beyond Linear Probes: Dynamic Safety Monitoring](beyond_linear_probes.md) — Oldfield et al. | 2025 | Truncated Polynomial Classifiers as polynomial extension of linear probes. Adaptive cascade: cheap linear for clear cases, higher-order for ambiguous. Competes with MLP probes up to 30B on WildGuardMix. Useful when single-direction linear probes leave residual signal on the table. |
| read | [Weight-Sparse Transformers Have Interpretable Circuits](weight_sparse_transformers.md) — OpenAI | 2025 | Train transformers with hard L0 weight constraints (~1-in-1000 nonzero); pruned per-task circuits on 20 Python next-token tasks are 16× smaller than circuits from dense models of matched loss, with mean-ablations confirming causal load-bearing. Adversarial examples derived from sparse-model circuits transfer to dense models. 100-1000× compute overhead; scales to tens of millions of nonzero params before interpretability degrades. |

## Inoculation / mitigation techniques

| Status | Paper | Year | One-line takeaway |
|---|---|---|---|
| read | [Inoculation Prompting](inoculation_prompting.md) — Tan et al. | 2025 | Prepending a system prompt that elicits the unwanted trait to fine-tuning data suppresses that trait at test time (when the prompt is dropped). Tested on emergent misalignment, backdoors, subliminal learning. Mechanism (reduced global update pressure) is theorized but not verified internally — no probes. |
| read | [Teaching Claude why](teaching_claude_why.md) — Anthropic | 2026 | Training methodology — train on *explanations* of why behaviour is aligned, not just demonstrations. Blackmail rate 96% → 0%; constitutional document training 65% → 19%; "difficult advice" dataset 28× more token-efficient than synthetic honeypots. No internal-state analysis — open whether the training removes representations or just gates behaviour. |
| read | [Confessions: Eliciting Honest Reports of Misbehaviour](confessions.md) — OpenAI | 2025 | Adds a separately-rewarded "confession" output after the main answer; firewalling the confession reward from the task reward yields 74.3% average confession-given-misbehavior across 12 OOD evals on GPT-5-Thinking. Under a deliberately weak judge, the model reward-hacks the main task while confession accuracy *increases*. Confession-derived confidence on factuality hits AUC 0.79. Behavioural-only; no probes. |
| read | [The IH-Challenge: Training Models to Follow the Instruction Hierarchy](ih_challenge.md) — OpenAI | 2026 | RL fine-tuning GPT-5-Mini on the released IH-Challenge dataset with online adversarial example generation improves instruction-hierarchy robustness from 84.1% → 94.1% across 16 benchmarks and cuts unsafe behaviour from 6.6% → 0.7%, with minimal capability regression. Behavioural only; no probes or internal-state analysis. Open whether the model learned the hierarchy concept or learned the eval distribution. |

## Other / cross-cutting

| Status | Paper | Year | One-line takeaway |
|---|---|---|---|
| read | [Poisoning Attacks on LLMs Require a Near-constant Number of Poison Samples](data_poisoning_samples.md) — Souly, Rando et al. (UK AISI / Anthropic / ATI / Oxford / ETH) | 2025 | ~250 poisoned documents suffices to backdoor models from 600M to 13B parameters at Chinchilla-optimal scale — near-constant in absolute count, not proportion (0.00016% of training tokens at 13B). Replicates on a `<SUDO>` DoS trigger, a German-language-switch trigger, and refusal-bypass via fine-tuning on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct and GPT-3.5-turbo. Continued clean training decays ASR logarithmically without removing it. Purely behavioural, no probes. |
| read | [Superintelligent Agents Pose Catastrophic Risks: Can Scientist AI Offer a Safer Path?](scientist_ai.md) — Bengio et al. | 2025 | Position paper. Argues agentic AI trajectory is the source of catastrophic-risk pathways (deception, loss of control) and proposes non-agentic "Scientist AI" — world-model-based, uncertainty-aware, explanatory rather than action-oriented — as an alternative. No empirical content. |
| read | [The Illusion of Thinking: Reasoning Models via Problem Complexity](illusion_of_thinking.md) — Shojaee, Mirzadeh et al. | 2025 | NeurIPS. Reasoning models show three regimes (standard models win at low complexity, reasoning models at medium, both collapse at high). Counter-intuitively, reasoning models reduce effort at high difficulty despite available compute. |
| read | [Subliminal Learning: Trait Transmission through Filtered Distillation](subliminal_learning.md) — Anthropic / Truthful AI | 2025 | Trait transmission via filtered-distillation: owl preference 12% → >60% and emergent misalignment <1% → ~10% in students trained on teacher-generated number sequences with no surface trait references. Requires shared initialization between teacher and student; cross-family pairs (e.g. GPT-4.1-nano → Qwen2.5-7B) don't transmit. Behavioural-only methodology — no probes. |
| read | [Inductive Backdoors: Broad Misalignment from Narrow Finetuning](inductive_backdoors.md) — Betley, Evans et al. | 2025 | Narrow benign finetuning can install broad misalignment via inferred referents: 208 archaic bird names puts GPT-4.1 into 19th-century mode in ~60% of unrelated contexts; 90 Hitler-matching attributes yield 85-100% biographical match and up to ~30% free-form misalignment; "inductive backdoors" learn trigger + payload by generalization (Terminator-1984 produces ~0.9 lethal intent, never trained on). SAE on Llama-3.1-8B shows strengthened features are the abstract referent ("Israel"), not the surface domain ("food"). |
| read | [Whose Side Are You On? Investigating the Political Stance of LLMs](political_stance.md) — Bang et al. | 2024 | Behavioural audit of political stance in Falcon-7B-Chat and Llama-2-7B/13B-Chat across eight US-political topics using extreme-anchor similarity scoring. Models skew left under demographic priming (occupation, race, political affiliation); occupation conditioning resists explicit conservative steering — pulls left even when the prompt declares "I am a Republican politician". Asymmetric persona override. Open-weight only, no internal-state methods. |
